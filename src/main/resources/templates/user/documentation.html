Basic Profile Information

On your Profile, use the edit [] functions to provide basic and contact information. A contact email address can be updated. The email address that was used to create the account is set as your user ID and cannot be modified. We recommend using 100-500 words to describe your research interests.

Additional Profile Information

You can also choose to provide:

  • A link to your home page (e.g., your lab website)
  • Research categories that apply to you
  • Contact email address that can be updated. The email address that was used to create the account is set as your user ID and cannot be modified.
  • A phone number
  • A summary of your research interests (we recommend 100-500 words)
  • A list of PubMed IDs for your publications
Human Organ Systems

You may select one or more human organ systems that your research is most relevant to. This information can be used when database searches are performed to select researchers with expertise in certain organ systems.

Additional Profile Categories

For each additional profile category, you can select one or more terms that your research is most relevant to. For simple categories, select terms by checking the appropriate boxes. For ontology categories, use the text box to search for terms of interest and add them to your profile by clicking on them.

Privacy Settings

Data Privacy Settings section lets you choose your global data sharing preferences. The default setting is pre-selected, but can be changed. The options are:

  • - your information will only be accessible by the network administrator for matching you to a new rare disease gene and considering you for funding opportunities.
  • - your information will be accessible by other researchers who are registered. Registered users will be able to access your information through the provided search interface.
  • - your information will be publicly accessible. If "shared" option is available, your information will be shared with registered users and will also be accessible to non-registered users through the registry’s public search webpage ().

Furthermore, this section gives you the option to set your preference for sharing your data with the network’s partner registries for the purposes of establishing connections between clinician sciences who are submitting their rare disease genes to partner registries and Canadian model organism/system researchers. The data shared with partner registries will only be visible to the administrators of those registries. If your data privacy level is set to "public", partner registries will gain access automatically.

To rapidly get a list of your PubMed IDs, in a new browser window use PubMed search to find your publications. The citations are returned 20 per page by default, but you can change this by clicking on "Display settings" and choosing a different setting for "Items per page". You can select the publications you wish to use by clicking on the checkboxes; to save all your citations do not click any check boxes. After making a selection click on "Display settings"; select "PMID List" and click "Apply". Copy and paste the resulting list of numeric IDs into the field in the Profile.

If your search returns a long list of publications and you would like to exclude only few of them click on "Send to", select Clipboard and click on "Add to Clipboard". In the top-right corner there will be a link to items in the Clipboard. Within the Clipboard you can remove any items you want and then export the PubMed IDs as described above.

Note: Currently the PubMed IDs are for future use and are optional.

After selecting a model system in the drop-down menu, you can optionally enter a description of your research on this system (System Research Focus), if you feel it is different from the description you may have provided in your Profile (About My Research).

The most important functionality of this section is the addition of genes, and then of Gene Ontology terms.

The main goal of the registry is to collect an accurate and comprehensive list of the genes that you study or are able to study.

Genes that you are entering directly into the system can be either Primary or Secondary (non-Primary).

  • Primary genes (Tier 1) are those you currently work on in a model system. You must be able to immediately, specifically and rapidly study them in your laboratory. Genes you have recently published on are especially good Primary genes. We expect this to typically be between 1-10 genes.
  • Secondary genes (Tier 2) are those you do not mark as Primary. Carefully choose genes which you are not necessarily actively investigating, but would be able to work on rapidly and specifically with minimal set-up time. These might be paralogs of Tier 1 genes, or members of the same complexes or pathway. For most registrants we expect there might be between 1-100 non-Primary genes entered.

For more information, see the relevant FAQ

In the input box below the table you may search genes one by one or type in a comma separated list of gene symbols. Add them by pressing the enter key or the "+" symbol.

The added genes will appear at the bottom of the table. You can remove genes by clicking the red X on the desired row. You can also change the Primary status of genes using the checkbox.

You may also choose a privacy levels for your genes that differ from the global privacy settings in your Profile. The new levels can only be more restrictive than your global privacy level. Options are:

  • private - this gene will only be accessible by the network administrator for matching you to a new rare disease gene and considering you for funding opportunities.
  • shared - this gene will be accessible by other researchers who are registered. Registered users will be able to access your information through the provided search interface.

Note that if you change the global privacy setting in your Profile, the genes will be updated to use the new value.

Finally, be sure to Save. If you navigate away from the page without doing so, you will be prompted to confirm.

Once you have added some genes, you can optionally provide some Gene Ontology (GO) terms related to your work. Genes associated with the GO terms you select will be considered as your Tier 3 genes. For more details about GO, please see geneontology.org.

To enter GO terms for an system, click on the "Terms" tab on the Model System Manager page. From here you can let us suggest some terms based on the genes you already entered by clicking on the "Recommend Terms" button or input terms manually using the input box below the table.

Note: You need to have at least two genes in order for us to successfully recommend terms.

Recommended GO terms will appear in a table (allow for a short delay especially if you have a lot of genes saved) that has the following columns:

  • GO term full name
  • GO Aspect, which indicates one of three domains from which the GO term originates (biological process (BP), molecular function (MF), cellular component (CC))
  • Overlap, which indicates how many of your (Tier 1 and 2) genes are annotated with that term. Clicking this number will show the specific genes.
  • Term Size, which indicates how many genes in the currently chosen model system are annotated with that term and will be added as Tier 3 genes if the term is selected (excluding the genes that are already added as Tier 1 or 2)

You may also manually search by GO ID, Name or description. Add them by pressing the enter key or the + symbol

Note: terms that have more than associated genes will not be available for addition to your Profile.

Once again, don't forget to Save.

If you have already entered at least two genes, the system can suggest related GO terms for you. The system uses a very simple algorithm to do this. We look at GO terms shared by the genes you have entered. If a term has enough genes overlapping with your genes, it is considered a candidate. We then prune terms that are too big, too small or highly redundant with another candidate term. A selection of the top candidates is then provided. The full process is a little more involved than this, as it makes use of the structure of the GO hierarchy and several heuristics to provide useful results. Please Contact Us if you would like more details.

The Search page allows you to search other registered users who chose to share their data publicly and/or with other users of the Registry. If "Include results from partner registries" option is selected, the registry will also connect to remote registries of the partners and search their "public" users. These results will be displayed in a separate section with the indication from which registry the matches are coming from ("Origin").

There are two ways to search for users: using the "Researcher" or "Gene" tab.

Searches on the "Researcher" tab

The search function on the Researcher tab uses various profile categories to search for registered users. You can search by name, key words in the user's research description or any other category that exists on the Profile page. The search will be preformed using a conjunctive normal form of the specified terms, meaning that all selected categories will need to be satisfied and at least one of the selected terms within each category. The applied search query will be shown at the bottom of the page once the search is performed.

After clicking on the Search button the list of results will be displayed at the bottom of the page. Clicking on the "View User Profile" button will open a new tab with the user's Profile and gene information.

Searches on the "Gene" tab

The Gene tab allows you to search for registered users based on genes they study. Gene symbols are system-specific and will be matched against the list of known gene symbols for the selected system (selection made in a dropdown menu). If you want to search for a match using a human gene, but consider orthologs in other model systems, you would use the secondary dropdown menu to indicate the preferred system.

The "Gene" tab allows you to search for registered users based on genes they study. Gene symbols are system-specific and will be matched against the list of known gene symbols for the selected system (selection made in a dropdown menu). If you want to search for a match using a human gene, but consider orthologs in other model systems, you would use the secondary dropdown menu to indicate the preferred system.

You can filter the results of your search using any of the categories displayed on the "Gene" tab page.

Filter by Human Organ Systems

You can also filter the results of your search by organ systems to restrict them to the users for whom these systems are relevant. If nothing is selected no filtering is performed.

Anonymous Results

If you lack permissions to see the matched researcher information (either their global privacy level or gene privacy level are set to "private"), their name and organization will not be displayed in the result table and the button on the left will say "Request Access" instead of "View User Profile". Clicking this button will take you to the a web form allowing to contact that user. The contacted researcher may choose to reply, in which case he/she will contact you via your contact email.
